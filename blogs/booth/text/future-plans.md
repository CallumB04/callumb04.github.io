# Future plans

I am currently building booth alongside my full-time role, and dedicating hours when I can, primarily as a learning project. However, from the original idea of booth, I believe this application would be extremely beneficial to many organisations and teams, and would like to eventually have it ready to be used by real-world teams, and potentially create a monetizable product.

My first plans, once the MVP is ready, is to begin integrating booth into the company that I work for. This will allow for real-world teams to use the software, find issues and provide feedback, whilst remaining low-risk as it is within the teams that I am collaborating with. This will also hopefully begin to solve the issues that inspired this idea in the first place, which will prove its use-case, and the need for the software.

As I mentioned before, I do not want to try and compete with the massive technologies and businesses that already dominate this space, and instead want booth to sit on top of them and integrate effectively. I would eventually like to create integrations with all major task-management software, to ensure booth can be implemented easily into all organisations, no matter what they are using internally.

Also, I would like to integrate with as many other technologies as possible, to allow for people to interact with booth from the comfort of what they are used to using. One idea for this, which I have mentioned already, is through chat bots in apps like Slack, Discord and Microsoft Teams. This will allow for people to message a 'booth bot' through their app of choice, and view the tickets in their task-management software of choice, without ever needing to open booth.
