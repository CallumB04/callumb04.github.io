# What problem is this solving?

The primary reason that I am building booth, is due to experiences in my Full-stack Web Development role, of which I am currently still in. This role was my first role in the Software Development industry, and has taught me a lot about collaborating across teams, with lots of different people, of varying technical understanding and ability.

One issue I noticed early on, is that different people with different backgrounds and experience, have their own language and way of saying things. This began to quickly cause me confusion, especially as someone with little professional experience, I didn't understand the business lingo being used, and could not always fully understand what I was being asked. On the other hand, from my perspective, people with less technical experience were not using the language that I was comfortable using - which paired with my lack of experience and poor technical -> non-technical language ability - led to further confusion on both sides.

As a Software Developer who is constantly receiving requests from all over the business (sales, delivery, etc), this problem needed a solution, which is where the idea for booth came from. I want to build an application that helps businesses and teams communicate, delegate and assign tasks, in the language that feels comfortable to them. booth will act as the middle-man, helping non-technical individuals translate their requests into actionable tickets, and providing a technical specification to the development teams that are assigned these tasks.

To further improve the easy usability of booth for non-techincal individuals, I plan to allow for a variety of methods for communicating these requests such as:

- A one-time message (for small requests)
- A back and forth conversation with an AI agent (for large / complex requests)
- Uploading of meeting transcripts from common technologies (Notion, Granola, etc)

I believe this will provide a self-intuitive interface that feels comfortable for everyone, and allow for booth to be implemented into teams of any technical level.
